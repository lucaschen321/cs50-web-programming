Chinese: Learning Chinese

Simplified vs. Traditional

Simplified Traditional
Speed of Writing
  • Simplified characters fewer strokes in general, which can make them faster to write
  • Traditional characters are often simplified ad-hoc to save time (e.g. 草書).
  • The speed of simplified Chinese is less relevant nowadays because of the conveniennce of input method editors (IME).
Aesthetics
  • Simplified Chinese characters are more legible when small fonts are used, as complex characters become harder to distinguish.
  • Traditional characters signify aesthetic continuity with Chinese heritage of art, literature, and calligraphy.
  • There is a strong preference for aesthetics of traditional characters among Mainland Chinese.
Practicality
  • Traditional Chinese characters are only still used primarily by those in Taiwan, Macau, Hong Kong.
  • Moving back to traditional chracters on mainland is potentially difficult, confusing, and time-consuming.
  • If the PRC was able to impose its scheme of simplified characters on the majority of Chinese people, then a reversion to the use of traditional characters could hardly be any less feasible.
  • Traditional characters are better suited for studying Chinese culture, since they more closely resemble written Classical Chinese characters.
Verdict: I have a vested interest in learning Traditional Chinese, since my family is from Taiwan. In addition, it's supposedly easier to learn Simplified Chinese, having acquired Traditional Chinese, than vice-versa.

Dictionary & Flashcard App

vs

Anki Pleco
Dictionary
  • Lack of a built-in dictionary makes adding new flashcards much more labor intensive.
  • Multiple high quality dictionaries available (plus easy import of user dictionaries).
Flashcards
  • More manual input, although there's more control over flashcard learning (e.g. number/duration of learning step)
  • More built-in Chinese support. You can OCR characters, draw them with your fingers, click them from the built-in reader, or just hit the + button on basically any screen anywhere in Pleco to add words.
Verdict: Pleco has a lot of advantages if you are specifically using flashcards to study Chinese words. If you want a single tool to study all kinds of flashcards, Anki is the better choice, but the overhead of building "notes" about Chinese words is much higher compared to Pleco.
